Summary: Suicide Rate Frequency Through Generation, Gender, Country, and Gross Domestic Product(GDP).


Purpose:

Suicide Rates have increased immensely for the past 30 to 45 years regardless of an influx of prevention methods. There is a major variability both geographically as well as with age and gender, in suicide rates. Most research has not clearly defined why there is so much variability within those three categories; however, the purpose of this study is to perform an exploratory analysis using D3.js visualization to visually depict the frequency of suicide rates over 30 years (1985-2016) to extract data and provide some potential answers to pondering questions from past research.


Methods:

Data was extracted from "Suicide Rates Overview" in Kaggle ('https://www.kaggle.com/russellyates88/suicide-rates-overview-1985-to-2016'). Data was cleaned through Pandas Jupyter Notebook. We dropped columns and numbers that were not pertinent to our research (i.e.,'HDI Rate' and zeros). Moving Forward, we created a database through SQL/PgAdmin4 where we formed tables that represented our new columns. Once we had our database, we created a html file that aggregated all of our individual apps for gender, generation, country, and GDP. Lastly we created our own individual Javascript and HTML Landing Pages for our website, then linked our SQL database and html pages to Flask for deployment.


Results:

The purpose of this project was to analyze how suicide rates have changed over time since 1985 to as recent as 2016. To accomplish this analysis, data was extracted from Kaggle and cleaned through SQL/PgAmin4 in order to assemble distinct graphs that show the frequency of suicide rates over 30 years through generation, gender (male vs female), and through 210 countries.
