Gross Domestic Product per Capita and Suicide Rates


How does the Gross Domestic Product per Capita of a country affect average suicide rates?

A linear regression was conducted using SciPy.stats to determine the correlation between suicide rate rates an population size in countries around the globe.


Africa

Based on the result of the linear regression, the R value was -0.7 and the p-value was 0.47. It can be determined that there is a strong negative correlation between average suicide rates and GDP per capita of countries in Africa . However, the p-value demonstrated that this negative correlation is not significant


Asia

Based on the result of the linear regression, the R value was 0.12 and the p-value was 0.61. It can be determined that there is a weak positive correlation between average suicide rates and GDP per capita of countries in Asia. However, the p-value demonstrated that this negative correlation is not significant.


North America

Based on the result of the linear regression, the R value was -0.19 and the p-value was 0.54. It can be determined that there is a weak negative correlation between average suicide rates and GDP per capita of countries in North America . However, the p-value demonstrated that this negative correlation is not significant.


South America

Based on the result of the linear regression, the R value was 0.37 and the p-value was 0.31. It can be determined that there is a weak positive correlation between average suicide rates and GDP per capita of countries in South America . However, the p-value demonstrated that this negative correlation is not significant.


Australia

Based on the result of the linear regression, the R value was 0.89 and the p-value was 0.107. It can be determined that there is a strong positive correlation between average suicide rates and GDP per capita of countries in Australia . However, the p-value demonstrated that this negative correlation is not significant.


Europe

Based on the result of the linear regression, the R value was 0.02 and the p-value was 0.89. It can be determined that there is a weak positive correlation between average suicide rates and GDP per capita of countries in Europe . However, the p-value demonstrated that this negative correlation is not significant.

In all, there is no significant correlation between GDP per capita and suicide rates in all continents.
