Gender and Suicide Rates


Which sex has a greater suicide rate?

According WHO's report men commit sucide at a higher rate than women. This analysis supports this claim as male suicide rates consist of 75 percent of the data, whereas female suicide rates consist of 25 percent of the data.

A T-test was conducted using SciPy.stats to understand whether the difference between female suicide rates and male suicide rates was significant. First, the T-Test was conducted using the sum of suicide rates in all continent based on sex. As seen in the snipet below, the p-value of the t-test was 0.28, which is greater than 0.05. This means that this sample shows that there is no significant difference between female suicide rates and male suicide rates based on continents. This is because this sample size is less than 35, which is affecting the p-value



Another T-test was conducted using SciPy.stats with the sum of suicide rates in 101 countries based on sex. As seen in the snipet below, the p-value of the t-test was 0.01, which is less than 0.05. This means that this sample shows that there is significant difference between female suicide rates and male suicide rates in all countries.
