Generation and Suicide Rates


Which generation has showed to be more at risk in the 6 continents?

The current study considered average suicide rates for Boomers, Generation X, G.I. Generation, Silent Generation, Genereration Z, Millenials. The graph below demonstrates the average suicide rates for each generation in 102 countries. The study also analyzed the average suicide rates between 1986 and 2016 in Africa, Asia, North America, South America, Australia and Europe.

Africa

In African countries, Boomers had the highest average suicide rates followed by the Generation X, Millenials, Silent, G.I. Generation, and Generation Z.



Asia

In Aisa countries, Boomers had the highest average suicide rates followed by the Silent, G.I. Generation, Generation X, Millenials, and Generation X.



North America

In North American countries, Boomers had the highest average suicide rates followed by the Silent, G.I. Generation, Generation X,, Millenials, and Generation Z.



South America

In South American countries, Boomers had the highest average suicide rates followed by the Generation X, Millenials, Silent, G.I. Generation, and Generation Z.



Australia

In Australia, Boomers had the highest average suicide rates followed by the Generation X, Silent, G.I. Generation, Millenials, Generation Z.



Europe

In European countries, Boomers had the highest average suicide rates followed by the Silent, G.I. Generation, Generation X, Millenials, and Generation Z.



A one-way ANOVA was conducted using SciPy.stats to understand whether the difference between suicide rates in all the continents is significant. As seen in the snipet below, the p-value of the t-test was 0.001, which is smaller than 0.05. This means that this sample shows that there is a significant difference in average suicide rates based on gemeration between all the continents.
