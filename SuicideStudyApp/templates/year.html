Year and Suicide Rates


What is the average suicide rates in 101 countries between 1985 and 2016?

A linear regression was conducted using SciPy.stats to analyze the correlation between suicide rates and year in the top 5 countries. The analysis was conducted between the year of 1985 and 2016.

First, the regression was conducted for the Russian Federation and it was found that there is a mild negative correlation (r-value = -0.634) between suicide rates and year. This means that as time goes on suicide rates in Russian Federation decreases. That decrease overtime is statistically significant because the analysis has a p-value (0.0003) less than 0.05.



The regression was conducted for the United States and it was found that there is a strong positive correlation (r-value = 0.832) between suicide rates and year. This means that as time goes on suicide rates in United States increases. That increase overtime is statistically significant because the analysis has a p-value (6.25e-09) less than 0.05.



The regression was conducted for the Japan and it was found that there is a medium positive correlation (r-value = 0.552) between suicide rates and year. This means that as time goes on suicide rates in Japan increases. That increase overtime is statistically significant because the analysis has a p-value (0.0012) less than 0.05.



The regression was conducted for the Republic of Korea and it was found that there is a strong positive correlation (r-value = 0.953) between suicide rates and year. This means that as time goes on suicide rates in Republic of Korea increases. That increase overtime is statistically significant because the analysis has a p-value (1.18e-16) less than 0.05.



The regression was conducted for the Ukraine and it was found that there is a weak negative correlation (r-value = -0.487) between suicide rates and year. This means that as time goes on suicide rates in Ukraine decrease. That decrease overtime is statistically significant because the analysis has a p-value (0.008) less than 0.05.
