Population and Suicide Rates


How does the population size of a country affect average suicide rates?

A linear regression was conducted using SciPy.stats to determine the correlation between suicide rate rates an population size in countries around the globe.


Africa

Based on the result of the linear regression, the R value was 0.9 and the p-value was 0.19. It can be determined that there is a positive correlation between average suicide rates and population size of countries in Africa . However, the p-value demonstrated that this positive correlation is not significant


Asia

Based on the result of the linear regression, the R value was 0.8 and the p-value was 1.17e-05. It can be determined that there is a significant positive correlation between average suicide rates and population size of countries in Africa . This signifies that as population increases in countries located in Asia so does the average suicide rates.


North America

Based on the result of the linear regression, the R value was 0.97 and the p-value was 1.72e-07. It can be determined that there is a significant positive correlation between average suicide rates and population size of countries in North America . This signifies that as population increases in countries located in North America so does the average suicide rates.


South America

Based on the result of the linear regression, the R value was 0.98 and the p-value was 1.72e-07. It can be determined that there is a significant positive correlation between average suicide rates and population size of countries in South America . This signifies that as population increases in countries located in South America so does the average suicide rates.


Australia

This signifies that as population increases in countries located in Australia so does the average suicide rates.
