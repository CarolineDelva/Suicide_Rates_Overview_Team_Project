Age and Suicide Rates


What is the average suicide rate in every age range in the six continents?

The Suicide Prevention Resource Center reports that between 2008 and 2017 people between the age of 45 - 64 years had the highest suicide rates and people between the age of 15 - 24 years had the lowest suicide rates per 100,000 in the United States.

The current study considered average suicide rates for people between the age of 5 - 14 years, 15 - 24 years, 25 -34 years, 55- 74 years and 75+ years. It analyzed the average suicide rates between 1986 and 2016 in Africa, Asia, North America, South America, Australia and Europe.

Africa

Victims in Africa between the age of 35 - 54 years had the highest average suicide rates followed by the age ranage 25 - 34 years, 15 - 24 years, 55 - 74 years, 75+ years, and 5 - 14 years.

Asia

Victims in Asia between the age of 35 - 54 years had the highest average suicide rates followed by the age ranage 55 - 74 years, 25 - 34 years, 15 - 24 years, 75+ years, and 5 - 14 years.

North America

Victims in North America between the age of 35 - 54 years had the highest average suicide rates followed by the age ranage 55 - 74 years, 25 - 34 years, 75+ years, 15 - 24 years and 5 - 14 years.

South America

Victims in South America between the age of 35 - 54 years had the highest average suicide rates followed by the age range 15 - 24 years, 25 - 34 years, 55 - 74 years, 75+ years, and 5 - 14 years.

Australia

Victims in Australia between the age of 35 - 54 years had the highest average suicide rates followed by the age range 25 - 34 years, 55 - 74 years, 15 - 24 years, 75+ years, and 5 - 14 years.

Europe

Victims in Europe between the age of 35 - 54 years had the highest average suicide rates followed by the age range 55 - 74 years, 25 - 34 years, 75+ years, 15 - 24 years, and 5 - 14 years.

A one-way ANOVA was conducted using SciPy.stats to understand whether the difference between suicide rates in all the continents is significant. As seen in the snipet below, the p-value of the t-test was 0.002, which is greater than 0.05. This means that this sample shows that there is a significant difference in average suicide rates based on age ranges between all the continents.
